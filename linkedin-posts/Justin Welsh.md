## Post #1 

Marrying the right person makes entrepreneurship a whole lot easier.

I think every entrepreneur out there would agree with me.

That's why it's so important to build a business that lets you invest healthy, quality time into your relationship.

It's not about grinding 24/7 while ignoring your family for the sake of profit.

Or being "heads down" in "just one more email" during dinner time.

The time you invest with your partner and family will come back to you 100x in patience, support, and love.

Here are a few tips that have helped my wife, Jennifer, and I build a business together:

1. Protect non-work time like it's a client meeting. 

We block off mornings for Wordle and coffee, and I keep work to four hours a day. 

When the time is on the calendar, it actually happens.

2. Keep the real numbers open and transparent.

Jennifer and I both know where the business stands financially, what's working, and what isn't. 

No surprises means we make decisions as a team instead of me dropping news on her.

3. Let your strengths divide the labor.

I write, and Jennifer edits every essay before it goes out.

Figure out what each of you is actually good at and build the work around that. 

The truth is, you don't have to choose between a thriving business and a thriving relationship.

I built mine to 8 figures, working four to five hours a day, four to five days a week, with no employees and no investors. 

My marriage got stronger in the process, not weaker.

It covers the systems, the boundaries, and the decisions that let you grow revenue while keeping the people you love at the center.

## Post #2

Because you're better than you think.

The only thing that matters is that you're getting slightly better every day.

Throw away all of the "overnight success" story nonsense you've trained yourself to believe is the status quo.

And remember that most people take decades to get traction in whatever they're working on.
