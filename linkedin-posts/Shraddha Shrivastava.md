## Post #1 


Everyone on LinkedIn is talking about one thing from PM Modi’s address:

“Work from home.”

But I think most people missed the actual signal.

This is not about another lockdown.
It’s about collective economic discipline.

If millions of people work from home even 1 day a week, 
the impact becomes massive.

Less traffic.
Less fuel consumption.
Less pressure on national resources.

One person doing it changes nothing.
Millions doing it together changes numbers.

It’s the same logic once spoke about:

If every Indian contributes even ₹1 collectively,
the outcome becomes enormous.

One remote day a week is a win-win for everyone:

• Employees save time and energy
• Companies reduce operational load
➕️
• Cities reduce congestion
• The nation saves resources collectively

Just like Anupam Mittal introduced one work-from-home day every week at Shaadi(dot)com, small collective shifts like these can create a much bigger national impact than we realise.

At my company, our team already works across different cities and countries, so remote collaboration has been a part of how we operate for a long time.

Maybe the future of work was never about offices vs home.

Maybe it was always about adaptability.
Agree?

## Post #2 

The biggest advantage today is not your degree. It’s your ability to LEARN without being told.

The people growing the fastest are not waiting for:

• a mentor’s permission
• another certification
• a company training
• the “right time”

They open YouTube.
Read newsletters.
Take courses.
Experiment.
Fail.

Repeat.

Self-learning is becoming the new superpower.

Because industries are changing too fast for traditional learning to keep up.

I’ve seen people with average backgrounds build extraordinary careers simply because they stayed curious longer than others.

And I’ve also seen highly qualified people become irrelevant because they stopped learning after college.

Your future income will depend less on what you know today…

and more on how quickly you can learn tomorrow.

The internet made knowledge free.

But discipline is still rare. ISN’T??
